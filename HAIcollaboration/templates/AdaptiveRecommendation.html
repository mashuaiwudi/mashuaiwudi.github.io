Welcome


Welcome to participate in this experiment! In this experiment, you will be presented with 40 people's profiles (including age, year of education, marital status, occupation, and work hours per week) one by one. Your task is to predict whether a person's income is > 50k USD per year or ≤ 50k USD per year.


  • In the first 20 tasks, you are required to make your own decisions. After 20 tasks, you will be invited to create some decision rules based on how you make decisions.

  • In the last 20 tasks, you will be provided with an AI assistant who will give a recommendation. You can decide whether to trust it.

    • The whole process will take you about 20 minutes. And we will pay a 1 USD bonus if your decision accuracy in the last 20 tasks rank top 10% in all survey participants.

    Note that we will conduct attention check.

    Below are some decision-making rules that are automatically generated based on the 20 tasks you have finished. It is not 100% accurate. And these rules might not be the same with the actual decision-making process in your mind. Now, you can change it or add new rules based on your actual decision processes.

    Each rule contains several conditions and a label. For example, below shows a rule [Age > 45 and EducationNum > 12: Label: > 50k] containing two conditions [Age > 45] and [EducationNum > 12] and a label [> 50k]. The relationship between all conditions inside a rule is AND, which means only if a person satisfies all conditions (Age > 45 AND EducationNum > 12) at the same time, you will predict the person's income to be (> 50k).

  • You can change any conditions in any rules by clicking the Drop-down menus and selecting a different value.
  • You can add a new condition in a rule by clicking the Add button. And you can create a new rule by clicking the Add Rule button at the bottom.
  • You can delete any conditions by clicking the Delete button. And you can delete a rule by clicking the Delete the whole rule button.
  • Once you finish creating a rule, you need to click the Check button. It will help you check whether your rule conflicts with your previous decisions. If a rule does not match your previous decisions, you may want to change the rule. Also, you can go on without changing it.
  • Note that the value is a multiple-selection, for example, if you want to set a condition "Occupation belongs to Executive managerial or Other service", you can select them one-by-one. The selected item will become Blue
  • If you want to cancel a selection, you need to click the item again to make its color back to Black

    • NOTE: if once you click the Check button, you are taken back to the beginning, this is because your browser does not support this feature. We are very sorry for this problem but you have to quit this study. Message us and we will give you a little compensation.

    To help you review and recall the 20 predictions you have made, you can click Last one and Next one to browse your historical decisions.
